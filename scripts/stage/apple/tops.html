More Details

The trick is to draw attention away from the midsection. Tops, with focus around the neck and shoulder area, to keep the attention to your bust and your slim limbs, and away from your middle, are ideal.

  • Embroideries and other pretty shoulder details (eg. sequins)
  • Tops with sleeve detail, like flares, slits, gathers, embroideries and cuff treatment will attract attention to your arms and away from your midsection.
  • Embellished necklines draw attention upwards.
  • Gathers and ruching, Wrap tops, mock wrap tops and tops with front knot detailing. The strategically placed folds create forgiving curvature over the midsection.
  • Heavy torso-wear necklines that open up your chest and face like V's, scooped necks, cowl necks, open shirt collars and boat necks because they elongate the torso.
  • Long tops that float away from your waist hip-length tops and tunic shirts. They help balance out your Apple body shape. If you have short legs, long tops will shorten them, so add heels.
  • Empire cuts that are gathered at the waist are fine when they lie flat in soft draping fabrics. As soon as the gathers pouf out too much they tend to accentuate the belly area.
  • Peplum tops.
  • Monochromatic looks are great as well, so try to go for a top and trouser / skirt in the same color.
  • Fabric-cord, linen, Thai silk, tweed, canvas, brocade and gabardine.
  • Swing tops and tops with a trapeze form
  • The length of your tops should catch you just below your hipbone or a few inches above crotch point when you wear jeans or pants.
  • Sometimes tucking tops into a fairly high rise pencil skirt has a flattering girdling effect, so don't shy away from tucking tops into skirts.
